Collections and Linear Independence

Definition. A collection of vectors uuu from VVV is linearly independent if and only if any (every) finite subcollection of uuu is linearly independent.

Ex. u={0,1,t,t2,...,tn,...}u=\{0,1,t,t^2,...,t^n,...\}u={0,1,t,t2,...,tn,...}. (infinitely sized collection of vectors)
The subcollection {0,t}\{0,t\}{0,t} is not linearly independent, so uuu is not linearly independent either.

Ex. u={1,t,1−t}u=\{1,t,1-t\}u={1,t,1−t}.
1(1)−1(t)+1(1−t)=01(1)-1(t)+1(1-t)=01(1)−1(t)+1(1−t)=0; therefore uuu is not linearly independent.
