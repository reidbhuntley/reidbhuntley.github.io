Geometric Applications

If AAA is a 2×22\times 22×2 matrix, the area of the parallelogram determined by the columns of AAA is ∣detA∣\mid\text{det}A\mid∣detA∣. This also applies to the volume of parallelopipeds for 3×33\times 33×3 matrices, and for higher-dimensional equivalents of parallelograms and volume for larger square matrices.

By determined by the columns of AAA, we mean the parallelogram that has (0,0)(0,0)(0,0) and each column of AAA as vertices. The remaning vertices can be determined from this information.

Let T:R2→R2T: \R^2\rightarrow\R^2T:R2→R2 be a linear transformation determined by the matrix AAA. If SSS is an object in R2\R^2R2 with finite area, then the area of T(S)T(S)T(S) is the product of ∣detA∣\mid\text{det}A\mid∣detA∣ and the area of SSS. (T(S)T(S)T(S) means applying TTT to every point in SSS.)
